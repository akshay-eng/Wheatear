# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Enterprise architects, platform/integration engineers, and technical decision-makers who
are evaluating or planning a migration between AI agent/orchestration platforms (Copilot
Studio, watsonx Orchestrate, OpenAI, Vertex AI, Bedrock AgentCore, n8n, and others). They
are technical and skeptical of hype — they're here to judge whether this is a credible
engineering project or vaporware, not to be sold to.

## Product Purpose

Wheatear is an open-source accelerator for migrating AI agents and workflows between
orchestration platforms, so a vendor or platform switch becomes a migration instead of a
full rebuild from scratch. The landing page exists to explain the problem (platform
fragmentation and lock-in are a present cost, not a future one), the architecture (a
canonical intermediate representation, a pipeline that's deterministic where correctness
matters and AI-assisted where intent has to be understood), and the current project status
honestly — then convert visitors into GitHub followers and contributors.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, narrative, trustworthy. Technical credibility without being dry or academic;
confident about the architecture, honest about being early-stage. Diagrams and concrete
artifacts (an IR snippet, a pipeline trace) do the persuading — not adjectives. Reads like
an engineer explaining a system to another engineer, not a marketing deck.

## Anti-references

- The generic SaaS-cream AI-template look: cream/sand background, gradient-clipped headline
  text, a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, identical icon+heading+text
  card grids repeated down the page.
- Corporate-bland enterprise software marketing: stock photography, navy-and-grey, faceless.
- Overly playful or cartoonish treatment — undermines credibility for infrastructure/migration
  tooling aimed at engineers making a real platform decision.

## Design Principles

- Show the architecture, don't just claim it — a real IR snippet or pipeline diagram earns
  more trust than a paragraph of adjectives.
- Be honest about stage. This is pre-v1. The design should read as "serious engineering
  project, early days," never as a polished product hiding its immaturity.
- Write for the technical reader first. They're evaluating, not being sold to — restraint
  beats hype every time there's a choice between them.
- One narrative thread, not a feature wall. Sections build a case in sequence; they aren't
  a disconnected grid of feature cards.
- Identity comes from the Northern Wheatear's actual migration story (Alaska to East
  Africa, ~14,500 km, the longest migration relative to body size of any songbird) used
  precisely — not generic nature/bird decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: contrast ratios (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text), full keyboard
navigation, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation on the page.
